Mitchell Lovett link

Mitchell Lovett is a Georgia attorney who practices in a variety of areas. Mitchell started Lovett Law after working as an associate for Baum, Glass, Jayne & Carwile, in the area of insurance defense.


Mitchell worked interning for both the Environmental Defense Fund in the areas of administrative and environmental law, and for a U.S. House Representative on the Energy Committee. Mitchell graduated from the University of Tulsa College of Law in Oklahoma. During his time in law school, Mitchell served as Supervising Editor for the Energy Law Journal, as an Officer for the LGBTQ+ outreach organization OutLaws, and as a member of Phi Alpha Delta legal fraternity. Mitchell holds a Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice and a minor in Political Science from Cameron State University.


Before attending the University of Tulsa College of Law, Mitchell served in the United States Army as a Paralegal for the JAG Corps and as a Special Agent for the United States Army Criminal Investigation Division Command (USACIDC). Mitchell has conducted felony investigations and protective service operations for DOD personnel both nationally and abroad. Mitchell Lovett represents clients across the greater Atlanta metropolitan area and Augusta, Georgia. After spending 10 years away from Georgia to serve in the U.S. Army and obtain his education, Mitchell is proud to call Atlanta his home.
